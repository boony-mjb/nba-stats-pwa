Basics of Rugby League
Game Structure

A match is played in two 40-minute halves, with a short break at half-time. The team with more points when time expires wins.

Scoring

A try (grounding the ball over the opponent's try line) is worth 4 points. The conversion kick afterward is worth 2, a penalty kick at goal is worth 2, and a drop goal during play is worth 1.

Tackles & The Six-Tackle Rule

The team with the ball gets six tackles to advance and score. If they're tackled a sixth time without scoring, possession switches to the other team.

The Play-the-Ball

After each tackle, the ball carrier gets up and rolls the ball backward with their foot to a teammate, restarting play without a stoppage.

Offside

Players on the defending team must stay behind the ball (or behind the back of a ruck/play-the-ball) until it's played, or they're penalised for offside.

The Ladder & Finals

Teams earn 2 points for a win and 1 for a draw across the regular season. The top 8 on the ladder qualify for a finals series, which culminates in the Grand Final.
