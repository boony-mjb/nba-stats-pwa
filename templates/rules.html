Basics of NBA Basketball
Game Structure

A game is split into four 12-minute quarters. If the score is tied after the fourth quarter, teams play a 5-minute overtime period — and another if needed — until someone's ahead.

Scoring

A made basket is worth 2 points inside the three-point arc, 3 points from beyond it, and a free throw following a foul is worth 1 point.

The Shot Clock

A team has 24 seconds to attempt a shot once it gains possession. Failing to do so turns the ball over to the other team.

Fouls & Free Throws

Personal fouls are called for illegal contact. A player who picks up 6 fouls in a game is disqualified and must leave the court. Certain fouls award the other team free throws.

Violations

Common violations include travelling (taking steps without dribbling), a double dribble (dribbling, stopping, then dribbling again), and goaltending (interfering with a shot on its way down toward the rim).

Playoffs

The top teams from each conference make the playoffs, which are played as best-of-seven series across four rounds, ending in the NBA Finals between the two conference champions.
