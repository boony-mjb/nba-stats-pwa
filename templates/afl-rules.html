Basics of Australian Football
Game Structure

A match is played in four 20-minute quarters (plus time added on for stoppages), with breaks between each. The team with the higher score at the final siren wins.

Scoring

Kicking the ball between the two tall centre posts is a goal, worth 6 points. Hitting a shorter post on either side, or the ball being touched before crossing the line, is a behind, worth 1 point.

The Oval Field & Open Play

AFL is played on a large oval field with no offside rule, so players can run and kick the ball anywhere on the ground at any time.

Moving the Ball

Players can kick or handball (punch the ball off one hand with the other) to teammates. After taking a mark or free kick, a player gets unimpeded time to play on.

Marking & Tackling

Cleanly catching a kick that's travelled at least 15 metres is a mark, awarding a free kick. Opponents can tackle the ball carrier, who must dispose of the ball correctly or risk being penalised for holding the ball.

The Ladder & Finals

Teams earn 4 points for a win and 2 for a draw across the home-and-away season. The top 8 on the ladder qualify for a finals series, which culminates in the AFL Grand Final.
